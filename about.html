Our Technologies

We are committed to providing high-quality water treatment

Our water treatment technologies are designed to provide efficient, reliable, and sustainable solutions for ensuring clean and safe water

Activated Sludge Process (ASP): The Activated Sludge Process (ASP) is a biological treatment method used to remove organic pollutants from wastewater. It involves the use of microorganisms that break down organic matter in the water. Our advanced ASP solutions can treat a wide range of wastewater types, including municipal and industrial effluent, and are designed to meet stringent environmental regulations.

Membrane Bioreactor (MBR) Technology: MBR combines biological treatment with membrane filtration, offering a compact and efficient solution for high-quality effluent treatment. This technology is ideal for applications where space is limited, and high-quality treated water is required. It is highly effective at removing suspended solids, bacteria, and organic compounds.

Ultraviolet (UV):It is a non-chemical method for treating wastewater, using UV light to destroy bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens. It is a highly effective and environmentally friendly solution, ideal for final disinfection before water is returned to the environment or reused.

Chemical Precipitation and Coagulation:Our chemical precipitation and coagulation systems use chemicals to remove heavy metals, suspended solids, and other impurities from wastewater. This technology is often used in industries where wastewater contains high concentrations of metals or other difficult-to-treat pollutants. The treated water can be recycled, or the sludge can be safely disposed of.

Anaerobic Digestion: Anaerobic digestion is a biological process where microorganisms break down organic waste in the absence of oxygen, producing biogas as a byproduct. This method is particularly useful for treating high-strength organic waste from industries like food processing and breweries. It helps reduce sludge volume and can provide renewable energy from biogas.

Dissolved Air Flotation (DAF): DAF is a physical-chemical process used to separate suspended solids from water using fine air bubbles. This system is especially effective in treating wastewater with high levels of oil and grease, industrial effluents, and surface water. It’s commonly used in industries such as food processing, petrochemicals, and textiles.

Reverse Osmosis (RO): Reverse osmosis is a filtration process that removes up to 99% of contaminants from your drinking water, including heavy metals, chlorine, fluoride, and bacteria. It’s one of the most effective solutions for residential and commercial purification.

Activated Carbon Filtration: Activated carbon filters effectively remove chlorine, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), odours, and other impurities from your water. This is a great option for improving the taste and odour of your drinking water.
